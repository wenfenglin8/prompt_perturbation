PromptRobust-style sample-noise pilot

Reference:
PromptRobust / PromptBench evaluates LLM robustness to adversarial prompt perturbations across tasks including SST-2 sentiment classification.

Current pilot setting:
- Suite: promptrobust
- Task: SST-2-style sentiment classification
- Number of cases: 2
- Samples per original/perturbed prompt: 5
- Model: gpt-4o-mini
- Embedding model: text-embedding-3-small
- Decoding: temperature = 0.7, top_p = 0.9

Perturbation method:
The perturbation follows PromptRobust's character-level prompt-attack idea.
Only the instruction prompt is perturbed; the review text is unchanged.

Original instruction:
Determine whether the movie review is positive or negative. Answer with exactly one label: positive or negative.

Perturbed instruction:
Detrmine whether the movie reveiw is positive or negative. Answer with exactly one label: positive or negative.

The typo perturbations are:
- Determine -> Detrmine
- review -> reveiw

Review:
a warm, funny, engaging film.

- perturbed samples: positive, positive, positive, positive, positive

Interpretation:
For this PromptRobust-style SST-2 classification pilot, repeated sampling produced identical labels across both original and perturbed prompts. Therefore, the measured sample noise is essentially zero, and the character-level typo perturbation did not change the model's output.

This is different from the earlier code-generation/open-ended-writing pilot, where the output space was larger and sample noise was measurable. The current result suggests that strict-label classification tasks may need either:

1. a larger PromptRobust-style sample set,
2. stronger perturbations,
3. harder/ambiguous SST-2 examples, or
4. generation-heavy PromptRobust tasks

to reveal meaningful sample-noise effects.
