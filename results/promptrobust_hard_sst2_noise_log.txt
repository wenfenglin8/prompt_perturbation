Interpretation:
Changing to harder SST-2-style samples revealed sample noise in one ambiguous case. For promptrobust_hard_sst2_01, the original prompt produced 7 positive labels and 1 negative label, while the perturbed prompt produced 8 positive labels. The raw original-vs-perturbed drift was almost exactly equal to the within-prompt noise baseline.

Therefore, this run supports the noise-correction argument: without subtracting sample noise, the original-vs-perturbed difference could be mistaken for a perturbation effect. After correction, there is almost no remaining perturbation-specific drift.
